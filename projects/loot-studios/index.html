Loot Studios

UX Strategy Consultant

2021 - 2023

Scope: Brazilian company

Environment: B2C e-Commerce Products · Business Expanding

After reaching their target number of subscriptions in just 18 months after launching, Loot Studios was ready to take the next step. The goal: transform their subscription-based website into a fully-fledged digital product that elevates the user experience and supports the business's expanding catalog.

Organizational Context

Brazilian company Loot Studios sells highly detailed 3D printable miniature STL bundles, released monthly and themed around fantasy and sci-fi RPGs. The community is extremely engaged, and their feedback offers invaluable insight into user needs and expectations.

As the freelance consultant UX Strategist for this project, I led the end-to-end process of defining the product vision, aligning cross-functional teams, and translating user insights into a clear roadmap and scalable digital experience. This created the perfect setting for a UX strategy initiative that would redefine how Loot's digital presence supports both users and business growth - not just for today, but as a foundation for the future.

The design process

Our UX strategy followed a clear, structured approach to ensure alignment, efficiency, and impact:

Activities undertaken

  • Immersion & Benchmarking

    Competitive analysis, Stakeholder interviews, Product & business review

  • User Research

    Community insights, 5 user interviews, Persona creation

  • Journey Mapping

    “As-is” experience mapping, Identification of pain points, Touchpoint analysis (before, on, and beyond platform)

  • Strategic Definition

    Value Proposition Canvas, Feature mapping & prioritization, MVP Canvas

  • Design & Prototyping

    UI designer hired & onboarded, Style guide and high-fidelity designs, Interactive prototypes

  • Information Architecture

    Content inventory & organization, Sitemap definition and Feature categorization

  • Usability Testing

    Structured testing plan, Iterative improvements, Validation of hypotheses

  • Launch Planning

    Soft rollout strategy, Feedback collection, Continuous improvement roadmap

What I Learned

  • Building a product vision requires more than solid research - it needs active alignment across teams and a careful balancing of user needs and business realities
  • Collaborative product strategy is just as critical as good design - involving business, design, and tech early ensures long-term success
  • A strong MVP definition (small but meaningful) empowers faster growth and continuous learning
  • When there is no internal product team, structured communication becomes one of the designer's most powerful tools
